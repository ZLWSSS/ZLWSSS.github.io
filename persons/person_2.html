 Shiyun is a second-year Ph.D. student in psychology at The Hong Kong Polytechnic University, specializing in studying adolescent internet use behaviors, with a keen focus on internet and gaming addiction disorders. Shiyun holds an M.A. in Educational Psychology from University College London and earned her bachelor's degree from Shenzhen University, where she completed a double major in Applied Psychology and Social Work. Her research aims to uncover the psychological traits that encourage digital well-being. Shiyun is particularly passionate about employing advanced quantitative methods, such as network analysis, SEM, and Latent Growth Mixture Modeling (LGMM), to analyze data and unearth patterns within complex behaviors. Her dedication to using these statistical techniques underscores her commitment to developing evidence-based strategies that can significantly enhance adolescents' ability to navigate the digital environment healthily.
